# Whānau Portal — Questions for Trustees

These questions need trustee decisions before the portal can be completed. Each one has a direct impact on how the site is built.

---

## 1. Whakapapa — Connecting to the Marae

**Background:** When someone registers on the site, they enter their name and the name of a parent. This links them into the family tree. The system currently only allows one parent connection.

**Questions:**
- Some people have two parents who both connect to the marae. How should this be recorded? Should the site allow two parent connections, or just one?
- If only one parent connection is recorded, what should that field be called? "Parent's Name" is generic — is there a better term that reflects the whakapapa connection to the marae?
- If someone's parent hasn't registered yet, their entry will sit unlinked in the tree until the parent registers. Is that acceptable, or should there be a way to manually link them?

---

## 2. Whakapapa — Who Can Edit Records

**Background:** The current rule is that you can only update the birth year or death year of yourself, your parent, your children, or your siblings. Anyone outside that circle cannot edit your record.

**Questions:**
- Is that the right boundary? Should it be wider (e.g. any whānau member can update any record) or narrower (e.g. only the person themselves)?
- Who records a death year when someone passes? Under the current rule, it would need to be done by a parent, child, or sibling of the deceased. Is that workable?

---

## 3. Announcements — Who Are the Trustees?

**Background:** Only designated trustees can post announcements on the site. Everyone else can read them. Trustees are flagged manually in the system by the administrator.

**Questions:**
- Who should be trustees (i.e. who can post announcements)? Please provide names and email addresses so they can be set up.
- Should trustees be able to delete announcements posted by other trustees, or only their own?
- Is there a maximum number of announcements to show, or should all past announcements remain visible?

---

## 4. Bookings — Notifications

**Background:** When someone makes a booking for the hall or meeting house, their contact details are stored but not shown publicly. Currently there is no email notification sent anywhere.

**Questions:**
- Should a notification email be sent when a booking is made? If so, to which email address?
- Does the marae have an email address for bookings, or does one need to be set up?

---

## 5. Domain Name

**Background:** The site is currently hosted at `https://mikeartee.github.io/ngati-pakahi-portal`. This is a temporary address.

**Questions:**
- Does the marae want its own domain name (e.g. `ngatipakahi.nz`)? If so, who will manage and pay for it?
- Is the current temporary address acceptable for the proof of concept period?

---

## 6. Pepeha

**Background:** A pepeha for the marae could be displayed on the home page or the login page, representing the identity of Ngāti Pakahi O Manga Iti.

**Questions:**
- Should a pepeha be displayed on the site? If so, where — the login page, the home page, or both?
- What is the agreed pepeha for Ngāti Pakahi O Manga Iti? This should be confirmed by the trustees before it is published.
- Should the pepeha be displayed in te reo Māori only, or with an English translation alongside it?

---
